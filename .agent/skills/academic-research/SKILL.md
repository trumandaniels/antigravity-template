# Academic Research & Synthesis Methodology

This skill executes a three-phase methodology for investigating any subject: precise retrieval, ruthless evaluation, and cohesive synthesis. 

## 1. Information Retrieval: Where and How to Search

To build a solid empirical foundation, bypass surface-level search engine results and target authoritative repositories.

* **Academic & Peer-Reviewed Databases:** Prioritize repositories like PubMed, JSTOR, IEEE Xplore, arXiv, and Google Scholar for primary research and methodologies.
* **Institutional Data:** Target domains ending in `.gov`, `.edu`, or `.org` (with scrutiny) for raw statistics, policy papers, and official reports (e.g., Census data, WHO, World Bank).
* **Advanced Querying:** Utilize Boolean logic (`AND`, `OR`, `NOT`) and search operators (`site:`, `filetype:pdf`, `"exact phrase"`) to filter out commercial noise and isolate high-signal documents.
* **Citation Chaining:** When a highly credible primary source is found, review its bibliography to trace the foundational literature (backward chaining) and check who has cited it recently to see modern consensus (forward chaining).

---

## 2. Credibility Evaluation: Separating Signal from Noise

Every source must pass a rigorous epistemological audit before inclusion. Do not take any text at face value.

### Source Evaluation Matrix

| Criterion | High Credibility (Keep) | Low Credibility (Discard/Flag) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Author Expertise** | Published academic, recognized industry expert, credentialed investigative journalist. | Anonymous, uncredentialed blogger, known bot/AI farm, "random person" on social media. |
| **Publication Venue** | Peer-reviewed journal, established university press, reputable journal of record. | Predatory journal, self-published blog, highly partisan think tank. |
| **Evidentiary Support** | Cites primary data, transparent methodology, explicitly acknowledges limitations. | Relies on logical fallacies, emotional appeals, unlinked claims, or fake quotes. |
| **Digital Provenance** | Website has transparent ownership and historical consistency (verifiable via Wayback Machine). | Recently registered domain, heavy ad saturation, lack of an editorial board. |

* **Detecting Bots & Fabrications:** Cross-reference sensational quotes against primary transcripts or academic archives to ensure they are not misattributed. Look for bot-like patterns in social media sources (e.g., hyper-frequency of posting, repetitive syntax, lack of biographical consistency). 
* **Lateral Reading:** Never evaluate a website solely by its own "About" page. Open new tabs to investigate what independent, authoritative entities say about that specific source or author.

---

## 3. Synthesis: Preparing Information for Downstream Use

Raw data is useless without a cohesive narrative. Process the validated findings into a structured, actionable format for the user.

* **Thematic Categorization:** Group findings by core themes, methodologies, or historical progression rather than simply listing sources sequentially.
* **Contradiction Mapping:** Highlight where experts disagree. A rigorous synthesis does not hide conflicting data; it explains the nuances, sample sizes, or variables causing the divergence.
* **Executive Abstraction:** Distill complex methodologies and datasets into clear, high-level summaries without losing technical accuracy or diluting the facts.
* **Absolute Traceability:** Ensure every synthesized claim maps back to a specific, evaluated source. This allows downstream users or agents to audit the chain of evidence.
